Six Reasons We Love Traveling With Rick Steves

“Six Raisons pour voyager avec Rick Steves”
“We learned a lot on the tour! We included a ton of tips, so you can relax and have as much fun as we did.”

We chose Rick Steves because we love how easy and relaxed it is, and because we had so many authentic, cultural experiences the last time we took a Rick Steves trip. This is consistent across both Rick Steve’s tours we have taken. Back in 2017, we went on the best trip we've ever taken, traveling around the UK on Rick Steves' Best of England & Wales tour. We had an amazing time, exploring cozy villages, and enjoying the beautiful city of Bath.

1. You’re Always With Friends
Everyone in the group gets to know each other, new friendships form, and there’s always someone who is up for a meal! The guides make an effort to get to know people on the tour, and are always fun and knowledgeable!

2. You Skip The Lines
All your museum tickets are booked in advance and you enter with a guide so there’s no waiting in the long queues that are often part of popular destinations.

3. Transportation Is Covered
The group often takes the bus or train together so you don’t need to worry about how to get around. Although Marie also taught us how to use the train on our own so we could navigate successfully on our free time and future trips.

4. Locals Shared Tips and Culture
Our tour guide and museum guides were all locals so we got insider tips on what foods to try, what times to go to popular attractions, the history of the neighborhoods, etc.

5. You Try New Things
Rick Steve’s encourages “no grumps” so everyone has the attitude of being open minded. This helps the group get a lot of done in a day and we all got to try lots of new foods and experiences.

6. Each Trip is Unique
Each tour is different based on the season, events, availability, etc. The guides always have a plan B and C! It didn’t happen often but occasionally weather or unexpected closures change the plan, but we always did something interesting.

Our Hotel

"Notre Hôtel"

Every tour is different, but on ours, we stayed at Hotel Londres Eiffel, a stylish boutique hotel in the 7th Arrondissement (district) of Paris, just blocks from the Eiffel Tower and Rue Cler, a beautiful shopping street full of tasty food and flowers. The staff were super helpful: Arnaud at the front desk recommended restaurants and helped us book taxis. The tour group hung out in the breakfast room every morning and even enjoyed drinks together in the lobby lounge one evening. We also had the pleasure of a rare visit from Samba, the hotel owner’s Golden Retriever (seen above!).
